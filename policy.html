Overview

Generative AI tools — including ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, and similar systems — are powerful aids for learning and productivity. This policy establishes clear boundaries for their use in my courses, balancing pedagogical value with academic integrity.

This policy reflects the evolving nature of AI in academia. It will be reviewed and updated each semester. Students are expected to be aware of the current version at the start of each course.

Bottom line: You may use AI as a thinking partner and learning aid. You may not use it to generate the substance of work you submit as your own. Disclosure is mandatory whenever AI was involved.

What is Permitted

The following uses of AI are acceptable in this course:

  • 01Using AI to brainstorm ideas, explore approaches, or break down a problem — as long as the final work reflects your own thinking and analysis.
  • 02Using AI to understand concepts or definitions you encounter in the course material, as a supplement to textbooks and lectures.
  • 03Using AI to debug code you wrote, or to explain error messages. The solution logic must remain yours.
  • 04Using AI to improve grammar or clarity of writing you have already drafted, without changing the substance or argument.
  • 05Using AI to generate example code that you then study, modify, and understand — with disclosure — for homework problems that explicitly permit this.

What is Prohibited

  • 01Submitting AI-generated text as your own written analysis, reasoning, or argumentation — even with minor edits.
  • 02Using AI to generate complete or near-complete solutions to homework problems or programming assignments.
  • 03Using any AI tool during in-class exams, quizzes, or assessments unless explicitly permitted for that specific assessment.
  • 04Using AI to translate between Korean and English when the assignment requires demonstrating proficiency in either language.
  • 05Failing to disclose AI use when it occurred. Undisclosed AI use is treated as academic dishonesty regardless of the extent of involvement.

Disclosure Requirements

When you use AI in any permitted way on a submission, include a disclosure statement at the end. Example:

AI Disclosure: I used ChatGPT (GPT-4) to brainstorm the structure of my answer in Problem 3. I then wrote the full response myself based on course materials. I also used Claude to check for grammatical errors in my final writeup.

Be specific: name the tool, describe how you used it, and explain what the final output reflects in your own work. Vague disclosures (“I used AI”) are not sufficient.

Consequences of Violation

Violations of this policy are treated as academic dishonesty. Consequences depend on severity:

  • ⚠Minor violation (e.g., using AI beyond permitted scope but disclosing): Zero on the assignment, verbal warning.
  • ⚠Major violation (e.g., AI-generated submission without disclosure): Failing grade for the course and report to the university honor committee.

Rationale

I permit AI use because I believe the ability to work effectively with AI tools is a genuine skill you will need in your career. Pretending these tools don't exist would be a disservice to your education.

I prohibit substituting AI for your own thinking because the goal of your education is to develop your ability to reason, analyze, and create. If AI does that work for you, you don't develop. The assessment is not the point — the learning is.

I require disclosure because honesty is foundational to academic trust, and because understanding how you used AI helps me improve assignments over time.
