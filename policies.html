POL — 01
AI & ChatGPT Use Policy
The use of generative AI tools such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Copilot is permitted in this course under specific conditions. Students may use AI for brainstorming, understanding concepts, and debugging code, but all submitted work must reflect the student's own understanding and analysis. Any AI-generated content must be clearly disclosed in a statement at the end of the submission, identifying which tool was used and how. Submitting AI-generated work without disclosure constitutes academic dishonesty and will be treated as plagiarism. In exams and in-class assessments, the use of AI tools is strictly prohibited unless explicitly stated otherwise by the instructor. These policies reflect the evolving landscape of AI in academia and will be updated each semester.

POL — 02
Grading Policy
Final grades are computed as follows: assignments (40%), midterm exam (25%), final exam (30%), and participation (5%). Letter grades follow the standard university scale (A: 90–100, B: 80–89, C: 70–79, D: 60–69, F: below 60). Late submissions will be penalized at 10% per day unless a written extension is approved before the deadline. Students may appeal a grade within two weeks of receiving it by submitting a written regrade request. Grade inflation is not practiced; the distribution reflects actual performance relative to course objectives.

POL — 03
Assignment & Collaboration Policy
Unless explicitly labeled as a group project, all assignments must be completed individually. You may discuss ideas and approaches with classmates at a high level, but the code, writing, and analysis you submit must be entirely your own. Do not share your solutions or code with other students. Plagiarism detection tools are used to compare all submissions. Copying from the internet, previous semesters' solutions, or other students is strictly prohibited and will result in a failing grade for the assignment and possible academic discipline.

POL — 04
Exam & Assessment Policy
All exams are closed-book unless stated otherwise. Students must arrive on time; no additional time is given to students who arrive late. Makeup exams are only permitted in cases of documented medical emergency or official university conflict notified at least 48 hours in advance. Accommodations for students with disabilities must be arranged through the university's disability office and submitted at least one week before the exam. Calculators, notes, and electronic devices are prohibited unless explicitly permitted.

POL — 05
Attendance & Participation Policy
Regular attendance is expected. Students may miss up to three lectures without penalty. Additional absences will result in a reduction of the participation grade (1% per absence beyond the first three). Absences due to illness, family emergency, or official university activities are excused if documented and submitted within one week. Participation includes in-class discussion, questions, and engagement with lecture material. Passive attendance does not constitute participation.

Office Hours & Communication Policy
Office hours are held Tuesdays and Thursdays, 3:00–4:30 PM in Engineering Hall 412. Email responses are provided within 48 hours on weekdays. Questions about assignments or course content should first be posted to the course discussion board so other students may benefit. Personal matters should be communicated via email with your student ID number in the subject line. I do not negotiate grades via email; if you wish to discuss grading, please attend office hours.
