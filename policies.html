These policies apply to all courses I teach unless a specific syllabus states otherwise. Please read carefully before the semester begins.

Featured Policies
POL — 01
AI & ChatGPT Use Policy
Generative AI tools such as ChatGPT, Claude, and Copilot are permitted under specific conditions. Students may use AI for brainstorming and debugging, but all submitted work must reflect the student's own understanding. AI-generated content must be disclosed. Submitting AI work without disclosure is academic dishonesty. In exams, AI is strictly prohibited.

POL — 02
Grading Policy
Final grades: assignments 40%, midterm 25%, final 30%, participation 5%. Standard scale (A: 90–100, B: 80–89…). Late submissions penalized 10% per day without an approved extension. Grade appeals must be submitted in writing within two weeks.

POL — 03
Assignment & Collaboration Policy
Unless labeled as a group project, all assignments must be completed individually. Discussing ideas at a high level is acceptable, but code and writing must be your own. Do not share solutions. Plagiarism detection tools are used. Violations result in a failing grade and academic discipline.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Makeup exams are only permitted in cases of documented medical emergency or official university conflict. You must notify me at least 48 hours in advance (when possible). Simply forgetting or misreading the schedule does not qualify. Contact me as early as possible with documentation.

  • Submit a written regrade request within two weeks of receiving the grade. Email a clear, specific explanation of which problem you believe was graded incorrectly and why. I do not negotiate grades verbally — all appeals must be in writing. Note that regrading may result in a higher or lower score.

  • Up to three unexcused absences are permitted without penalty. Each additional absence reduces your participation grade by 1%. Absences due to illness, family emergency, or official university activities are excused if documented and submitted within one week.

  • Slides are generally posted on Google Classroom the morning of class. Recording policies vary — please check the individual course syllabus. Reference materials and readings are posted at least one week in advance.

  • Late submissions are penalized 10% per calendar day unless a written extension has been approved before the deadline. Extensions are granted for genuine emergencies with documentation. Please ask before the deadline, not after.
